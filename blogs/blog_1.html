6 tricks to make a good first impression

Non verbal skills play an extremely important role in creating a first impression. You make your first impression upon someone within 7-30 seconds of meeting that person.

Here are some tips to make a positive first impression:

1) Smile - It's contagious! This is the oldest & the easiest trick in the book. Everyone likes a smiling face. A simple genuine smile always makes other feel good.

2) Be attentive While talking or listening to anybody always maintain an appropriate eye contact. You should be on the same page as the other person. For example if someone smiles at you, you can always smile back. If you agree with what the other person's saying then you can always nod.

3) Speak clearly You don't have to be too loud or too quiet, it makes you look dominating or shy. Researches have shown that people who speak in a calm, deep voice are taken far more search than the ones who are too loud or too unclear. So always speak calmly, clearly and efficiently.

4) Engage & be engaging The most common mistake that people make while trying to make a favourable first impression is to continuously flaunt their knowledge. You should rather make efforts to strike an engaging conversation with the other person. Do listen to what the other person has to say.

5) Your posture It's completely normal to feel anxious & nervous when you are meeting someone new. But you don't have to show it. You body language should always be calm, composed & comfortable. •You should always keep your back straight. Do not slouch. •Your chin should be parallel to the ground. Neither too high as it shows arrogance now to low as it reflects lack of confidence & low self esteem. •Do not cross your arms for too long. It shows lack of interest in the conversation.

6) Be on time This one is absolutely non-negotiable. You should never be late. As it's easier to create a good first impression than to correct a bad one. Rather you should try to be a little early. As it will buy you some time to relax & calm your mind.
